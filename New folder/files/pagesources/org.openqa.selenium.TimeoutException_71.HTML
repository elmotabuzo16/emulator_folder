Chapter 1. General provisions

Article 1. Overview of the Terms and Conditions
1. The Vitality programme is a programme that aims to encourage the Member to actively engage in improvement of his/her health and general well-being through the use of benefits including discounts, cash back and others relating to goods and/or services offered by Insurer and its partners, based on the achievements of the prescribed activities.
2. The Vitality programme awards the Vitality points (hereinafter referred to as "Points") to the Member for certain activities, determines the Vitality status level (hereinafter referred to as "Status") for those activities, and offers benefits to the Member according to the status, as prescribed in the Terms and Conditions, the Vitality website and the Vitality mobile app terms and conditions, ●(Guide for Earning Points (tentative title) and the benefit guide (hereinafter collectively referred to as "Terms and Conditions and Guides").
3. The Vitality programme will become available for use by Member on and after the date of activation (which refers to the first day of the month following the month containing the day on which Insurer's liability for the eligible insurance policy starts (hereinafter referred to as "Issue date"). In the case where the eligible insurance policy referred to in the Article 2 is converted and the policy after conversion is regarded as the eligible insurance policy, the Issue date for the policy before conversion is regarded as the date of activation after the conversion. The same applies hereinafter.)
4. The Member is advised to regularly visit the Vitality Member Portal (hereinafter referred to as "Member Portal") exclusive for the Members using the Internet browser or the Vitality mobile app, or Insurer's home page for details of Vitality programme and the latest version of the Terms and Conditions and Guides.
5. The Member shall comply with the Terms and Conditions and Guides and the provisions set out by partners for use of Vitality programme.
Article 2. Membership
The Member must be an individual of at least eighteen (18) years of age and be the insured for an eligible insurance policy (which is an insurance policy concluded between the Insurer and the Member (including a policy after conversion) and to which the rider "Health Improvement Rate Applicable Rider" is added. The same applies hereinafter).
Article 3. Non-transferrability of the  Membership
1. The Vitality membership is personal and limited to the Member himself or herself, and the Membership, points, status, benefits and other rights related to them (in this article hereinafter referred to as "Membership") may not be transferred, hypothecated nor otherwise disposed（in this article hereinafter referred to as "Transfer").
2. Notwithstanding the provisions in the preceding paragraph, when points or benefits are transferred to the third party (including the members other than the Member himself or herself. The same applies in this Article hereinafter), neither transferrer nor transferee may earn the points or use the benefits.
3. The Member shall not make a third party use Vitality programme.
4. The Member shall be liable for any damage to the Insurer or the Partner that may result from the transfer of the Membership by the Member.
Article 4. Membership number
1. The Member shall be assigned with a Vitality membership number (hereinafter referred to as "Membership number").
2. Benefits will become available for use by the Member on and after the date of activation. However, use of certain benefits may take a prescribed procedure by the Insurer or the Partner or certain period of waiting time.
3. If the Member is requested by a Partner to present his or her membership number, policy number of the eligible insurance policy, or partner membership card specified by the partner but fails to do so, in some cases the Member may not earn points nor use a benefit.
4. The Membership number, the ID/password for the Member Portal and other numbers, sign or symbol for Member identification (hereinafter referred to as "Membership ID") shall be used exclusively by the Member relating to the Membership ID.
5. The Insurer shall believe that all the instructions and the transactions carried out using the Membership ID were done by the Member himself or herself and shall not be liable for damages to the Member or a third party that result from them, unless the Insurer has previously been notified by the Member of an unauthorized use of the Membership ID (including suspected use) or there is any other reason attributable to the Insurer.
6. The Member shall comply with the followings.
(1) Not to use the Membership ID for any commercial, fraudulent or unlawful purposes.
(2) To immediately notify the Insurer of any unauthorised use, loss or theft of the Membership ID to the contact point set out in the Article 25.
(3) To change password immediately if there is any reason to suspect that the security and/or confidentiality of the password has been compromised.

Chapter 3. Points and Status

Article 8. Earning points
1. The Member will earn the points by engaging in activities prescribed in the Guide for Earning Points (tentative title) (hereinafter referred to as "Activities") following these terms and conditions and regulations of the partners on and after the date of activation.
However, for certain activities, partners set additional conditions for earning points. In some cases Insurer apply such additional rules that differentiate the number of points that can be earned by an identical activity depending on the Member's age, gender, health status and other attributes as prescribed in Guide for Earning Points. Also, for certain activities, a limit is imposed on the number of points that can be earned in certain periods of time.
2. The Member shall confirm the number of the earned points on the Member Portal.
3. In some cases, it may take certain periods of time for the points to be reflected on the Member Portal. The Insurer is not responsible for any damages to the Member caused by the belated or inaccurate reflection of points due to reasons not attributable to the Insurer.
4. The Member is advised to immediately notify the Insurer of any error recognized in the points reflected to the Member Portal within eighty (80) days from the date on which the eligible activity was engaged in to the contact point set out in the Article 25. If the Insurer does not receive any notifications from the Member within the stipulated period, the number of points will not be corrected, and the number of points reflected to the Member Portal for the Member are deemed to be correct.
5. In the case where the number of points are corrected as set forth in the provision of the first half of the preceding paragraph, if the error in the number of points is due to the reasons not attributable to the Insurer, the Member shall not claim benefits retrospectively from the date on which the activity was engaged in.
6. For more information on points, please refer to the benefit guide on the Member portal or Insurer's home page.
Article 9. Validity of Points
1. The points reflected to the Member Portal will be reset to zero on the last day of the membership year (This refers to a period of time reckoned from the anniversary date of activation (which is the day that corresponds to the date of activation each year) to the day on which 1 year has elapsed. The same will apply hereinafter) in which the points are earned. No points will be carried over into the subsequent membership year.
2. Excluding the cases where the points are automatically reflected to the Member Portal, the Member must claim the points earned by an activity within six (6) months from the date on which the activity was engaged in. If the points are not claimed within this period, the points will not be awarded for the activity.
3. In the case where the points are claimed during the period referred to in the preceding paragraph, if the membership year containing the day on which the activity is engaged in and the membership year containing the day on which the claim is made differ, the points will be deemed to have been earned in the membership year containing the day on which the activity is engaged in. It is noted that the Member shall not claim use of benefits retrospectively from the date on which the activity was engaged in.
4. If any claim for points is made until the day on which two (2) months have elapsed from the date of the insurance premium determination (This refers to the day that is six (6) months reckoned from the anniversary of the issue date for the insurance policy in each membership year. The same applies hereinafter) in the membership year containing the day on which the Member engaged in the activity, the points for the activity will be reflected to the status which is used to determine the insurance premium amount for the membership year following the membership year containing the day on which the activity was engaged in.
5. If any claim for points is made within six (6) months from the day on which the Member engaged in the activity and after two (2) months have elapsed from the date of insurance premium determination in the membership year containing the day on which the Member engaged in the activity, the points for the activity will be reflected to the status which is used to determine the insurance premium amount for the membership year two (2) years after the membership year containing the day on which the activity was engaged in.
Article 10. Status
1. The status is determined from the predefined levels, which are Blue, Bronze, Silver and Gold, according to the number of points accumulated throughout the membership year (This means the number of points reflected to the Member Portal. It is the number of points after deduction of points to be forfeited as set out in the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 15 or the fourth paragraph of Article 19, and hereinafter referred to as "Accumulated points".) The points required for each status is as indicated in the table below, and the number of points upon Vitality programme activation is 0 (zero, the status of Blue).
Status Points
Blue 0-11,999 points
Bronze 12,000-19,999 points
Silver 20,000-23,999 points
Gold 24,000 pts or more
2. The status to be applied for use of benefits and the status to be applied to determine insurance premium amount for the insurance policy are as follows.
(1) Status used for the use of benefits
This is the status based on the number of the accumulated points at the time when the use of benefits is claimed. However, in the case where the status at the time of claim is lower than the Member's status on the last day of the previous membership year, the status on the last day of the previous membership year is taken.
(2) Status used for the insurance premium amount determination
This is the status in the determination period as prescribed in Health Improvement Rate Applicable Rider. However, in the case where the status in the determination period for the said rider is lower than the Member's status on the last day of the previous membership year, the status on the last day of the previous membership year is taken.
3. For the details about the relationship between each status and the terms of use for benefits and others, please refer to the benefit guide on the Member portal or Insurer’s home page.

Chapter 4. Termination of the Contract

Article 11. Lapse and reinstatement of the Contract
1. If the Member fails to pay the membership fee in two (2) months in a row, the Contract will lose efficacy by the expiration of the last day of the second month (hereinafter referred to as "Lapse").
2. In the period of three (3) months reckoned from the date of lapse of the Contract (hereinafter referred to as "Grace period"), the Member may reinstate the Contract having obtained approval from the Insurer, if the eligible insurance policy is valid and payments of the membership fees have been completed for the time period to the lapse and during the lapsing period.
3. If the Contract is reinstated based on the provisions in the preceding paragraph, the Lapse prescribed in the first paragraph is deemed not to have occurred. For the information on the handling of the points during the period in which the Contract is not valid, please refer to the Guide for Earning Points.
4. If the Member has not reinstated the Contract during the Grace period, the Contract will be terminated on the expiration of the Grace period.
5. Whether the membership fees have been paid or not, if the eligible insurance policy lapsed as prescribed in the policy clauses, the Contract will also lapse simultaneously.
6. If the eligible insurance policy and the Contract both have lapsed, they need to be reinstated simultaneously. Neither of the insurance policy nor the Contract shall be reinstated alone.
 Article 12. Withdrawal of applications
1. The Member may withdraw the application by means of document until the day on which eight (8) days have elapsed from the date on which the Insurer issued the document  relating to recall of application or cancellation of the Contract (hereinafter referred to as "Withdrawal of applications"), or from the date of application for the Contract, whichever is later.
2. Withdrawal of applications based on the provisions in the preceding paragraph shall take effect when the Member issues a document relating to the withdrawal of the application.
3. The Member who has withdrawn the application based on the provisions in the first paragraph may claim refund of the membership fees that have already been paid to the Insurer. However, in some cases, refund by the Insurer shall not happen due to the limitation that corresponds to the amount calculated by multiplying the value which is obtained by dividing the amount of membership fees received by the Insurer by thirty (30) days, by the number of days from the date of activation for Vitality programme to the date of recall of the application.

Article 1. Overview of the Terms and Conditions
1. The Vitality programme is a programme that aims to encourage the Member to actively engage in improvement of his/her health and general well-being through the use of benefits including discounts, cash back and others relating to goods and/or services offered by Insurer and its partners, based on the achievements of the prescribed activities.
2. The Vitality programme awards the Vitality points (hereinafter referred to as "Points") to the Member for certain activities, determines the Vitality status level (hereinafter referred to as "Status") for those activities, and offers benefits to the Member according to the status, as prescribed in the Terms and Conditions, the Vitality website and the Vitality mobile app terms and conditions, ●(Guide for Earning Points (tentative title) and the benefit guide (hereinafter collectively referred to as "Terms and Conditions and Guides").
3. The Vitality programme will become available for use by Member on and after the date of activation (which refers to the first day of the month following the month containing the day on which Insurer's liability for the eligible insurance policy starts (hereinafter referred to as "Issue date"). In the case where the eligible insurance policy referred to in the Article 2 is converted and the policy after conversion is regarded as the eligible insurance policy, the Issue date for the policy before conversion is regarded as the date of activation after the conversion. The same applies hereinafter.)
4. The Member is advised to regularly visit the Vitality Member Portal (hereinafter referred to as "Member Portal") exclusive for the Members using the Internet browser or the Vitality mobile app, or Insurer's home page for details of Vitality programme and the latest version of the Terms and Conditions and Guides.
5. The Member shall comply with the Terms and Conditions and Guides and the provisions set out by partners for use of Vitality programme.
Article 2. Membership
The Member must be an individual of at least eighteen (18) years of age and be the insured for an eligible insurance policy (which is an insurance policy concluded between the Insurer and the Member (including a policy after conversion) and to which the rider "Health Improvement Rate Applicable Rider" is added. The same applies hereinafter).
Article 3. Non-transferrability of the  Membership
1. The Vitality membership is personal and limited to the Member himself or herself, and the Membership, points, status, benefits and other rights related to them (in this article hereinafter referred to as "Membership") may not be transferred, hypothecated nor otherwise disposed（in this article hereinafter referred to as "Transfer").
2. Notwithstanding the provisions in the preceding paragraph, when points or benefits are transferred to the third party (including the members other than the Member himself or herself. The same applies in this Article hereinafter), neither transferrer nor transferee may earn the points or use the benefits.
3. The Member shall not make a third party use Vitality programme.
4. The Member shall be liable for any damage to the Insurer or the Partner that may result from the transfer of the Membership by the Member.
Article 4. Membership number
1. The Member shall be assigned with a Vitality membership number (hereinafter referred to as "Membership number").
2. Benefits will become available for use by the Member on and after the date of activation. However, use of certain benefits may take a prescribed procedure by the Insurer or the Partner or certain period of waiting time.
3. If the Member is requested by a Partner to present his or her membership number, policy number of the eligible insurance policy, or partner membership card specified by the partner but fails to do so, in some cases the Member may not earn points nor use a benefit.
4. The Membership number, the ID/password for the Member Portal and other numbers, sign or symbol for Member identification (hereinafter referred to as "Membership ID") shall be used exclusively by the Member relating to the Membership ID.
5. The Insurer shall believe that all the instructions and the transactions carried out using the Membership ID were done by the Member himself or herself and shall not be liable for damages to the Member or a third party that result from them, unless the Insurer has previously been notified by the Member of an unauthorized use of the Membership ID (including suspected use) or there is any other reason attributable to the Insurer.
6. The Member shall comply with the followings.
(1) Not to use the Membership ID for any commercial, fraudulent or unlawful purposes.
(2) To immediately notify the Insurer of any unauthorised use, loss or theft of the Membership ID to the contact point set out in the Article 25.
(3) To change password immediately if there is any reason to suspect that the security and/or confidentiality of the password has been compromised.

Chapter 3. Points and Status

Article 8. Earning points
1. The Member will earn the points by engaging in activities prescribed in the Guide for Earning Points (tentative title) (hereinafter referred to as "Activities") following these terms and conditions and regulations of the partners on and after the date of activation.
However, for certain activities, partners set additional conditions for earning points. In some cases Insurer apply such additional rules that differentiate the number of points that can be earned by an identical activity depending on the Member's age, gender, health status and other attributes as prescribed in Guide for Earning Points. Also, for certain activities, a limit is imposed on the number of points that can be earned in certain periods of time.
2. The Member shall confirm the number of the earned points on the Member Portal.
3. In some cases, it may take certain periods of time for the points to be reflected on the Member Portal. The Insurer is not responsible for any damages to the Member caused by the belated or inaccurate reflection of points due to reasons not attributable to the Insurer.
4. The Member is advised to immediately notify the Insurer of any error recognized in the points reflected to the Member Portal within eighty (80) days from the date on which the eligible activity was engaged in to the contact point set out in the Article 25. If the Insurer does not receive any notifications from the Member within the stipulated period, the number of points will not be corrected, and the number of points reflected to the Member Portal for the Member are deemed to be correct.
5. In the case where the number of points are corrected as set forth in the provision of the first half of the preceding paragraph, if the error in the number of points is due to the reasons not attributable to the Insurer, the Member shall not claim benefits retrospectively from the date on which the activity was engaged in.
6. For more information on points, please refer to the benefit guide on the Member portal or Insurer's home page.
Article 9. Validity of Points
1. The points reflected to the Member Portal will be reset to zero on the last day of the membership year (This refers to a period of time reckoned from the anniversary date of activation (which is the day that corresponds to the date of activation each year) to the day on which 1 year has elapsed. The same will apply hereinafter) in which the points are earned. No points will be carried over into the subsequent membership year.
2. Excluding the cases where the points are automatically reflected to the Member Portal, the Member must claim the points earned by an activity within six (6) months from the date on which the activity was engaged in. If the points are not claimed within this period, the points will not be awarded for the activity.
3. In the case where the points are claimed during the period referred to in the preceding paragraph, if the membership year containing the day on which the activity is engaged in and the membership year containing the day on which the claim is made differ, the points will be deemed to have been earned in the membership year containing the day on which the activity is engaged in. It is noted that the Member shall not claim use of benefits retrospectively from the date on which the activity was engaged in.
4. If any claim for points is made until the day on which two (2) months have elapsed from the date of the insurance premium determination (This refers to the day that is six (6) months reckoned from the anniversary of the issue date for the insurance policy in each membership year. The same applies hereinafter) in the membership year containing the day on which the Member engaged in the activity, the points for the activity will be reflected to the status which is used to determine the insurance premium amount for the membership year following the membership year containing the day on which the activity was engaged in.
5. If any claim for points is made within six (6) months from the day on which the Member engaged in the activity and after two (2) months have elapsed from the date of insurance premium determination in the membership year containing the day on which the Member engaged in the activity, the points for the activity will be reflected to the status which is used to determine the insurance premium amount for the membership year two (2) years after the membership year containing the day on which the activity was engaged in.
Article 10. Status
1. The status is determined from the predefined levels, which are Blue, Bronze, Silver and Gold, according to the number of points accumulated throughout the membership year (This means the number of points reflected to the Member Portal. It is the number of points after deduction of points to be forfeited as set out in the provisions of the first paragraph of Article 15 or the fourth paragraph of Article 19, and hereinafter referred to as "Accumulated points".) The points required for each status is as indicated in the table below, and the number of points upon Vitality programme activation is 0 (zero, the status of Blue).
Status Points
Blue 0-11,999 points
Bronze 12,000-19,999 points
Silver 20,000-23,999 points
Gold 24,000 pts or more
2. The status to be applied for use of benefits and the status to be applied to determine insurance premium amount for the insurance policy are as follows.
(1) Status used for the use of benefits
This is the status based on the number of the accumulated points at the time when the use of benefits is claimed. However, in the case where the status at the time of claim is lower than the Member's status on the last day of the previous membership year, the status on the last day of the previous membership year is taken.
(2) Status used for the insurance premium amount determination
This is the status in the determination period as prescribed in Health Improvement Rate Applicable Rider. However, in the case where the status in the determination period for the said rider is lower than the Member's status on the last day of the previous membership year, the status on the last day of the previous membership year is taken.
3. For the details about the relationship between each status and the terms of use for benefits and others, please refer to the benefit guide on the Member portal or Insurer’s home page.

Chapter 4. Termination of the Contract

Article 11. Lapse and reinstatement of the Contract
1. If the Member fails to pay the membership fee in two (2) months in a row, the Contract will lose efficacy by the expiration of the last day of the second month (hereinafter referred to as "Lapse").
2. In the period of three (3) months reckoned from the date of lapse of the Contract (hereinafter referred to as "Grace period"), the Member may reinstate the Contract having obtained approval from the Insurer, if the eligible insurance policy is valid and payments of the membership fees have been completed for the time period to the lapse and during the lapsing period.
3. If the Contract is reinstated based on the provisions in the preceding paragraph, the Lapse prescribed in the first paragraph is deemed not to have occurred. For the information on the handling of the points during the period in which the Contract is not valid, please refer to the Guide for Earning Points.
4. If the Member has not reinstated the Contract during the Grace period, the Contract will be terminated on the expiration of the Grace period.
5. Whether the membership fees have been paid or not, if the eligible insurance policy lapsed as prescribed in the policy clauses, the Contract will also lapse simultaneously.
6. If the eligible insurance policy and the Contract both have lapsed, they need to be reinstated simultaneously. Neither of the insurance policy nor the Contract shall be reinstated alone.
 Article 12. Withdrawal of applications
1. The Member may withdraw the application by means of document until the day on which eight (8) days have elapsed from the date on which the Insurer issued the document  relating to recall of application or cancellation of the Contract (hereinafter referred to as "Withdrawal of applications"), or from the date of application for the Contract, whichever is later.
2. Withdrawal of applications based on the provisions in the preceding paragraph shall take effect when the Member issues a document relating to the withdrawal of the application.
3. The Member who has withdrawn the application based on the provisions in the first paragraph may claim refund of the membership fees that have already been paid to the Insurer. However, in some cases, refund by the Insurer shall not happen due to the limitation that corresponds to the amount calculated by multiplying the value which is obtained by dividing the amount of membership fees received by the Insurer by thirty (30) days, by the number of days from the date of activation for Vitality programme to the date of recall of the application.
